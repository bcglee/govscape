Description
Arizona fescue is a native, cool season, long lived
perennial bunch grass. The plant has no rhizomes.
The culms or stems can range from about 1 to 3 feet
tall, clustered, pale blue-green, and are rough to the
touch. Leaf blades are mostly basal, pale blue-green,
6 to 10 inches long, shaped like a string, and rough to
the touch. The inflorescence is a panicle about 3-8
inches long. It has a deep, dense fibrous root system.
